THE DISTINCTION
There are two completely different situations involving documents:
Situation A — Someone is sending ME a document to read
Examples:

A psychologist sends their assessment report for my information
Allied health sends a progress note
A GP sends a discharge summary
A school sends a student report
Pathology sends results
Any email where the document is attached or described as being provided to the clinician

These are incoming documents — FYI only.

Classify as NONE or PROFESSIONAL depending on sender
No document task created
No 20 minute time addition
Time estimate stays at the category default — 1 to 2 minutes to read and acknowledge
Show a small badge: "📄 Document received — for your information"
The only action offered is "Mark as read" and optionally "Add a note"


Situation B — Someone is REQUESTING that I write or complete a document
Examples:

A school asks me to complete a support letter
A family requests an NDIS report
A GP asks for a referral letter
A court requests a medico-legal report
An insurer asks for a certificate
Any email where the sender is asking the clinician to produce something
Key language: "please complete", "please provide", "we need a report", "could you write", "please fill in", "requesting a letter", "can you send us"

These are outgoing document requests — action required.

Apply the full document task creation logic from the previous spec
Add 20 minutes to the time estimate
Create a linked task automatically
Show badge: "📄 Document requested — task created"


HOW TO TELL THE DIFFERENCE
The AI should use the following logic when it detects a document:
Step 1 — Check the direction
Ask: is a document being sent TO the clinician, or is a document being requested FROM the clinician?
Signals that it is incoming (Situation A):

Email has an attachment described as a report, letter, or results
Language like "please find attached", "I am sending you", "for your information", "for your records", "please see attached", "I wanted to share"
Sender is a colleague, allied health, GP, school, or pathology service sending their own work

Signals that it is outgoing request (Situation B):

Language like "please complete", "please provide", "we need", "could you write", "requesting", "can you send us", "we would appreciate a letter"
Sender is asking for something the clinician needs to produce
No attachment, or attachment is a blank form to be filled in

Step 2 — If unclear
If the direction cannot be determined confidently, treat it as Situation A (incoming, FYI) but add a small prompt to the email: "Is this asking you to write something? [Yes — create a task] [No — just information]"
