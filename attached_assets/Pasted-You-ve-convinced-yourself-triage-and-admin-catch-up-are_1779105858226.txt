You've convinced yourself triage and admin catch-up are semantically different, and I agree — they really are. Triage is "scan for things that escalated, decide what's urgent." Admin catch-up is "work through the queue of notes/correspondence." Different cognitive tasks, different time scales (15-20 min vs 30-60 min), different value if they go uncompleted (missing triage = clinical risk; missing admin = stress + backlog).
The argument against splitting them is just "fewer concepts to track." But once we add it, we'll never want to remove it, because the UI can render them as two distinct pills/blocks on the recovery day, which is genuinely clearer for the clinician.

What changed
RecoveryConfig now has a fourth array, triageReservedMin: number[]. Default [20, 0, 0] — 20 minutes on day 1 back, nothing on days 2 and 3.
DailyAvailability carries a new field triageReservedMin: number next to recoveryReservedMin. Both are protected slots the planner can never pack into.
On a typical day 1 back, the picture is now:

90 minutes available (50% ramp)
60 minutes admin catch-up (working through note backlog)
20 minutes triage scanning (quick scan for things that escalated)
10 minutes actually free for new scheduled work

That's correctly tight — day 1 back shouldn't be a normal work day, and now the planner knows why the day is busy rather than just seeing a generic reserved blob.
For the planner-side wiring
When you brief your builder on connecting this up, the rule for triage is identical to admin catch-up. Same treatment everywhere:

Step 1 of planner: unpack triageReservedMin into the internal day record alongside recoveryReservedMin and lowQuotaRemainingMin.
Step 6 packing: triage minutes are never consumed by any item. Not by urgent. Not by overdue. Nothing.
Step 7 status: count both reserved buckets toward "claimed capacity" when computing day status.

The UI can render them as two pills on the recovery day — "Triage 20 min" and "Admin catch-up 60 min" — which is more informative than a single 80-min generic block.
