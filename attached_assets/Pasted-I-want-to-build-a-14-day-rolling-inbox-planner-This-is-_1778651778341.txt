I want to build a 14-day rolling inbox planner. This is the core feature of the app. Everything else supports this. Please read all existing planning and classification code first, then implement the following in full.

THE CORE CONCEPT
The clinician has emails arriving constantly. Every email has a deadline based on its priority. The clinician has limited admin hours each week. The planner's job is to answer one question every morning:
"Given everything in my inbox, everything coming in this week, and the hours I have — here is exactly what to do today so that nothing breaches and I stay on top of everything."

WHAT THE PLANNER KNOWS
Current inbox:
All emails currently in the inbox with their category, priority, time estimate, and date received.
Projected incoming workload:
Based on historical patterns, the clinician receives approximately 60 emails per week on average. The expected breakdown is:

5 high priority (SAFEGUARDING, URGENT_CLINICAL, LEGAL)
10 medium priority (CLINICAL, PROFESSIONAL)
Remainder low priority or no action (ADMIN, NONE, CPD, LOW, UNCLEAR)

The planner must reserve capacity for these projected emails — not assume the week only contains what is already in the inbox. Specifically, reserve:

1.5 hours per week for projected high priority emails
1 hour per week for projected medium priority emails
30 minutes per week buffer for unexpected items

Available admin time:
Entered by the clinician — total hours and which days. Example: 5 hours across Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday.
Deadlines by priority:

SAFEGUARDING: same day or within 24 hours — always first
URGENT_CLINICAL and LEGAL: within 48 hours
CLINICAL and PROFESSIONAL: within 5–7 days
ADMIN, CPD, LOW: within 14 days of receipt
NONE: within 14 days but lowest priority
UNCLEAR: must be triaged before any other work — see below


THE DAILY PLAN LOGIC
Each day's plan is built using this logic in this exact order:
Step 1 — Unclear emails first
If any UNCLEAR emails exist, they appear at the very top of today's plan before anything else. The clinician must classify them before the plan continues. Show: "You have X unclassified emails. Please review these first — they may contain urgent items."
Step 2 — Overdue items
Any email that has already passed its deadline appears next. These are urgent regardless of original priority. Show how many days overdue each one is.
Step 3 — Due today or tomorrow
SAFEGUARDING, URGENT_CLINICAL, and LEGAL emails due within 48 hours.
Step 4 — Medium priority progressing
A portion of CLINICAL and PROFESSIONAL emails, working through them steadily so none approach their 5–7 day deadline without being handled.
Step 5 — Daily low priority allocation
Every single day, include at least 2–3 low priority emails in the plan. This is non-negotiable. Low priority emails must be cleared steadily every day — not batched at the end of the week. The planner should allocate approximately 15–20 minutes per day for low priority and no action emails, regardless of how many urgent items exist.
Step 6 — Linked tasks
Any task linked to an email in today's plan appears directly below its email as one combined item.
The plan should contain no more than what fits in that day's available admin time. If there is more work than time, carry the remainder to the next available day — but always keep the Step 5 low priority allocation even if it means deferring some medium priority items.

   ~5 high priority arriving — reserved 1h 30min
   ~10 medium priority arriving — reserved 1h

This is the most important honest signal the app can give. A clinician who thinks they have 5 hours but actually only has 2 hours of free capacity will run into problems every week without ever understanding why.

Implement this as a hard rule in the scheduling logic:
Every day that has any admin time must include at least 15 minutes of low priority email clearing, regardless of how many urgent or medium items exist.
If the clinician skips the low priority portion of today's plan, show a gentle reminder the next day:
"You skipped 3 low priority emails yesterday. They have been added to today's plan. None are urgent yet — but 2 will approach their 14-day window by end of this week."

UNCLEAR TRIAGE — MORNING GATE
If any UNCLEAR emails exist when the clinician opens the app:

Show a prompt before anything else: "You have X emails that need classifying. Until these are classified, your plan may be incomplete — one of them could be urgent."
Show the unclear emails one at a time with a simple category selector
Once classified, the plan recalculates immediately and includes them in the correct priority order
If the clinician dismisses the unclear prompt without classifying, show a reminder every 2 hours


RECALCULATION
The plan recalculates automatically when:

The clinician marks an email as done
The clinician marks a task as done
A new email arrives and is classified
The clinician changes their available hours
The clinician defers an item to a later day

Every recalculation shows the updated status indicator immediately.

FEEDBACK TRIGGERS
Show a feedback message in these situations:
If available hours are consistently insufficient:
"Over the last 3 weeks you have needed an average of 1h 20min more than you scheduled. Consider booking 6 hours instead of 5 next week."
If low priority emails are consistently skipped:
"You have 14 low priority emails older than 10 days. At current pace, 6 will breach the 14-day rule this week. Today's plan includes 5 of them."
If unclear emails are consistently dismissed:
"You have dismissed the classification prompt 4 times this week. 3 of your unclear emails are now 3 days old."
If the inbox is growing week on week:
"Your inbox has grown by 12 emails over the last 2 weeks. Your current admin time is not keeping pace. Recommend adding 1 hour per week or reviewing what can be delegated."
